Computer Science and Social Entrepreneurship workshop

Learn

Code for Good is a project-based workshop for high school students with a passion for technology and social impact. Students identify needs in their community and form a social enterprise. They learn HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Python through creating a web presence for their organization.

Download Syllabus

Curriculum

Community Engagement

According to Ashoka, Social Entrepreneurs are "individuals with innovative solutions to society's most pressing social problems". Students will adapt the mindset of a social entrepreneur by researching the problems that affect their communities and develop a social enterprise that addresses these issues. They will create a mission and business plan for their organization.

Module 1

Learn to Code

Students will create personal profiles using HTML,CSS, and JavaScript. They will learn principles of User Experience design to develop webpages that are easy to use and visually appealing.

Module 2

Create an Online Presence

Social Media expert Sean Gardner once said “Social media is not just an activity; it is an investment of valuable time and resources.” Students will develop a website for their social enterprise and use social media to engage their target audience.

Module 3

Add features

Students will learn Python to learn programming concepts and implement additional features and functionality to their social enterprise websites.

Module 4

Presentations

Students will develop presentation skills and demo their projects to community members and leaders.
